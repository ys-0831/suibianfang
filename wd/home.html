END HUNGER, ACHEIVE FOOD SECURITY & IMPROVED NUTRITION & PROMOTE SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE
Hunger is the leading cause of death in the world. If we promote sustainable agriculture with modern technologies and fair distribution systems, we can sustain the whole world’s population and make sure that nobody will ever suffer from hunger again.
Target Actions Things To Do
TARGETS
#1
End Hunger and Ensure Food Access
Ensure everyone, especially the poor and vulnerable, has access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round by 2030
#2
End Malnutrition
End all forms of malnutrition by 2030, focusing on children under 5, adolescent girls, pregnant and lactating women, and older persons. Achieve internationally agreed targets on stunting and wasting by 2025.
#3
Maintain Genetic Diversity of Seeds and Animals
By 2020, preserve the genetic diversity of seeds, cultivated plants, and farmed animals through well-managed seed and plant banks, ensuring fair and equitable sharing of benefits from genetic resources.
#4
Increase Investment in Agriculture
Enhance investment in rural infrastructure, agricultural research, technology development, and gene banks to boost agricultural productivity in developing countries, especially the least developed.
#5
Correct and Prevent Trade Restrictions
Eliminate agricultural export subsidies and similar measures to correct and prevent trade distortions in world agricultural markets, in line with the Doha Development Round.
#6
Ensure Proper Functioning of Food Markets
Implement measures to ensure food commodity markets function properly, providing timely access to market information to help limit extreme food price volatility.
THINGS TO DO
Find a Goal 2 charity you want to support. Any donation, big or small, can make a difference!
Buy local and in-season food. Try to grow some of your own food.
Buy Funny Fruit—many fruits and vegetables are thrown out because their size, shape, or color are not “right”. Buying these perfectly good funny fruit, utilizes food that might otherwise go to waste.
Don’t waste food. Freeze fresh produce and leftovers if you don’t have the chance to eat them before they go bad.
Consume less meat and become vegetarian for one day a week. The meat production industry has a huge impact on the environment.
Stay informed. Follow your local news and stay in touch with the Global Goals online or on social media at @TheGlobalGoals.
